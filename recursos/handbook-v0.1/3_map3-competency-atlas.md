# Competency Atlas

> [!quote] What abilities am I developing, and how confident am I in using them?

> i think the competences still can get better, but a like this one

## Purpose
- The Competency Atlas tracks personal growth throughout the Medical Robotics Journey.
Instead of measuring progress through completed courses, grades, or time spent studying, it focuses on the development of practical and intellectual abilities.
Its objective is to provide an *honest picture of current strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities* for improvement. It also helps identify whether progress requires acquiring new knowledge or strengthening confidence through practice.
## Description
- The Competency Atlas is organized around **competencies**, not subjects.
A competency represents an ability that can be applied to solve engineering, scientific, or medical robotics problems.
Each competency contains two independent measurements:
- **Mastery** — how developed the competency is.
- **Confidence** — how secure you feel applying it.
These two measurements evolve independently and should always reflect your current understanding.

## Rules
### Rule 1
Each competency represents an ability, not a university subject.
### Rule 2
Evaluate yourself honestly.
The objective is improvement, not high numbers.
### Rule 3
Mastery and confidence are different.
High mastery with low confidence is normal.
High confidence with low mastery is a warning sign.
### Rule 4
Update competencies after meaningful learning experiences, not after every study session.
### Rule 5
Whenever possible, support evaluations with evidence.
Examples:
- completed project
- solved problems
- implemented algorithm
- explained concept
- research paper read
- presentation given
### Rule 6
Competencies should connect to the Question Journey.
If an Act requires a competency, it should be visible here.
### Rule 7
Competencies should evolve gradually.
Large jumps are uncommon and should be justified by significant achievements.
### Rule 8
Do not compare competencies with other people.
The Atlas measures personal growth.
### Rule 9
Review competencies periodically.
Growth often becomes visible only after several months.
### Rule 10
Every competency should include a next objective.
Growth should always have a clear direction.

## Mastery Scale
### Level 0 — Unknown
"I have never studied this."
### Level 1 — Awareness
"I recognize the terminology."
### Level 2 — Understanding
"I understand the basic ideas."
### Level 3 — Application
"I can solve guided problems."
### Level 4 — Independence
"I can solve new problems without guidance."
### Level 5 — Integration
"I can connect this competency with other areas and teach it."
### Level 6 — Innovation
"I can contribute new ideas, methods, or research."

## Confidence Thresholds

⭐☆☆☆☆: Very insecure
⭐⭐☆☆☆: Some confidence
⭐⭐⭐☆☆: Comfortable
⭐⭐⭐⭐☆: Very confident
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐: Completely comfortable

Notice that confidence is **subjective**, and that's okay. Its purpose is to guide where you might need more practice or reinforcement.
