# Dependency Graph
> [!quote] How is knowledge connected, and what should I learn before or after a concept?
## Purpose
- The Dependency Graph organizes knowledge according to logical relationships rather than chronological order.
Its purpose is to make learning transparent by showing *how concepts depend on one another* and what opportunities they unlock. Instead of guessing whether you're ready to study a topic, the graph provides a visual explanation of the knowledge path.
The Dependency Graph also encourages long-term thinking. Every concept becomes part of a larger network rather than an isolated destination.
## Description
The Dependency Graph is a *network of concepts*.
Each concept acts as a node connected to other nodes through different types of relationships. These relationships describe *prerequisites, consequences, and natural learning progressions*.
Unlike the Question Journey, which is linear, the Dependency Graph is non-linear. There are often multiple valid paths through the same body of knowledge.
The graph should evolve continuously as new relationships become apparent.
## Structure

```
Concept
↓
Prerequisites
↓
Core Idea
↓
Unlocks
↓
Related Competencies
↓
Related Domains
↓
Related Questions
```

Notice that the concept sits in the center.
Everything else revolves around it.
## Rules
### Rule 1
Every concept must connect to at least one other concept.
Knowledge should never exist in isolation.
### Rule 2
Dependencies should represent understanding, not course order.
Just because a university teaches Topic A before Topic B doesn't necessarily mean A is a prerequisite.
### Rule 3
Prefer simple, direct dependencies.
Avoid unnecessarily long chains.
### Rule 4
Whenever possible, explain _why_ a dependency exists.
Understanding the relationship is more valuable than memorizing it.
### Rule 5
A concept may have multiple prerequisite paths.
Engineering problems rarely have only one route.
### Rule 6
Each concept should indicate what it unlocks.
Learning should always feel like opening new possibilities.
### Rule 7
Dependencies may evolve as understanding improves.
The graph is expected to change over time.
### Rule 8
If a concept feels difficult, consult the graph before assuming the concept itself is the problem.
The missing piece is often a prerequisite.
### Rule 9
The graph should remain readable.
Favor clarity over completeness.
### Rule 10
The graph is a guide, not a restriction.
Alternative learning paths are encouraged when appropriate.
## Relationship Types (Version 0.1)

### Requires
This concept cannot be properly understood without the previous one.
### Supports
This concept makes another easier to understand but is not absolutely required.
Control theory can be learned mathematically first, but physics provides intuition.
### Unlocks
Learning this concept opens the door to more advanced ideas.
## Relationship with the Other Maps
### Question Journey
The Question Journey tells you **why** you're learning a concept.
### Medical Robotics Atlas
The Atlas tells you **where** that concept belongs in the field.
### Competency Atlas
The Competency Atlas tells you **how well** you've developed the ability to use it.
